Why Your Support Matters

The Barba Azul Nature Reserve safeguards a unique ecosystem, including the world's largest population of the Critically Endangered Blue-throated Macaw.

Barba Azul protects 12 additional threatened fauna species of the Beni Savanna, as recognized by the IUCN, including the Endangered Dwarf Tinamou and the Vulnerable Jaguar, Giant Anteater and Buff-breasted Sandpiper.

Your support helps protect not only this remarkable macaw but the entire ecosystem it depends on with more than 350 bird species.

Why Your Donation Counts

Your monthly support of $25, or even more, creates a direct impact at Barba Azul Nature Reserve.

Protect the Reserve

Support Park rangers who prevent illegal hunting, parrot trapping, logging, and fishing.

Prevent Wildfires

Maintain 60 km of firebreaks, conduct controlled burns, train staff, and defend the savanna from destructive fires.

Restore Motacú Palms

Reforest key feeding areas, monitor palm growth, and expand nursery capacity to ensure macaws have the fruit they need to raise their young.

Advance Research

Monitor Blue-throated Macaws, Buff-breasted Sandpipers, and key mammals, and engage students and researchers in field conservation.
